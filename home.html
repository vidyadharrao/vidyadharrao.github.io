My research intrestes span in the area of computer science, algorithms and statistics. Broadly, I work on solving problems in Machine Learning, Optimization, Computer Vision and Data Privacy.
  • As an undergrad researcher, I have worked in privacy preserving algorithms for a collaborative anti-spam filtering system; ontological system for semantic annotation of events via Attention Markers in video recordings of Cricket Sport; and attempted to enable semantic search and retrieval via Generative Graph Grammars.

  • As a graduate researcher, I have worked on sublinear time algorithms that search for diverse nearest-neighors via randomized hash functions in multi-media retrieval; extreme multi-label (with long-tail distributions) low-rank matrix decomposition learning to retrieve diverse and relevant labels for images and texual documents; semi-supervised algorithms for learning distance metrics to improve k-means clustering, and diversity in instance retrieval; geometry and vision algorithms to estimate structure of the environment pictured in a given set of images, and utilize vision as a source of information to recognize objects, people, and activities in images. I have also worked on problems in cryptography like secure transmission of a message between two legitimate users over an insecure communication channel.

  • My non-academic research work spans across multi-disciplinary domains such as learning semantics for latent factors for recommendation systems in cold-start and data sparity contexts; analysing unstructured enterprise data for compliance enforcement that enables data protection and privacy of Personally Identifiable Information (PII); and develop efficient machine-vision algorithms for low-resource (data, compute) on-premises environments to improve productivity in industrial automation and control systems.
